ACT I: THE CROWN OF UJJAIN

The Throne of Ujjain

Bhaskaracharya — Karnataka, 1114 CE

Born in 1114 CE in Vijjadavida, Karnataka, Bhaskaracharya — 'Bhaskara the Teacher' — inherited a dynasty of mathematicians. His father Mahesvara was an astrologer. He learned mathematics at his father's knee, and in time became the head of the greatest astronomical observatory in all of India: the legendary Ujjain observatory — successor to Brahmagupta himself.

HISTORICAL INSIGHT: He stood at the peak of 600 years of Indian mathematical tradition — Aryabhata → Brahmagupta → Mahaviracharya → Bhaskaracharya.
