ACT I: THE SELJUK EMPIRE
   
       

Nishapur, Cradle of the Polymath

       

Khorasan, Persia — c. 1048 CE

       

In the great trading metropolis of Nishapur — jewel of the Seljuk Empire — a child was born on May 18, 1048. His family name, Khayyam, meant 'tent-maker' in Arabic. But this boy would stitch together the vast tents of mathematics, astronomy, and poetry into one magnificent mind.

   
   
        HISTORICAL INSIGHT: Nishapur was one of the largest cities in the medieval world, a crossroads of Greek, Indian, and Persian knowledge.
